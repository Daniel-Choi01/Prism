Why Prism exists

A reflection instrument for the moments you're too close to see clearly.

When something is weighing on you, you're trapped inside one point of view — your own, in the middle of the feeling. Advice from others collapses everything into their single answer. Prism does the opposite: you say what's on your mind, and it reflects it back — the feeling, and the belief underneath — weighing the angles you're too close to see, like white light opening into a spectrum. And because it remembers what you've reflected on before, it can name the thread running across your weeks that a fresh chat never could.

It never tells you what to do. It reflects, and it asks. The decision stays yours — you just get to see it whole first.

Powered by Claude (Anthropic). Your reflection is sent to a private endpoint, never shown to anyone else, and saved only if you choose to. Built for the SMG Bubble Cohort 001 “Reflection” Hackathon.

Privacy & your data

Prism is built private-first. Here's exactly what happens to what you write — in plain language.

Your words stay on your device
Your reflections are kept privately in your own browser so Prism can show you patterns over time. Nothing is uploaded unless you choose “Save & copy link” to make one shareable — and even then it's reachable only by that private link, with no public list or feed. Wipe everything anytime with “Delete everything” below.
The AI key never touches your browser
Your reflection is sent to a secure server function that talks to the AI. Your device never holds any secret key, and requests aren't cached or indexed.
Nothing is sold or shared
Your reflections are never sold, advertised against, or shared with anyone. Feedback you send is just a rating and an optional note — never the content of what you reflected on.
Help improve Prism with my reflections Off by default. When on, your saved reflections may be reviewed privately to make Prism better. Turn it off and that never happens — guaranteed. You're in control either way.

Why Prism isn't just another chatbot

The fair question any reviewer asks: why not just ask ChatGPT the same thing? Here's the honest answer.

Because a fresh chat answers your question. Prism answers you — grounded in everything you've reflected on before, built to ask rather than decide, and honest about exactly what it is.

The honest comparison
A typical AI chat
Prism
Memory
Starts from zero every time. It forgets you the moment the tab closes; it can't see a pattern across your weeks.
Remembers you. Every reply is grounded in your own past reflections and recurring themes, and a Patterns view names what keeps surfacing over time.
What it's for
Optimized to give you the answer — to resolve, recommend, and move on.
Optimized to hand the decision back. It reflects the feeling, notices the belief underneath, and asks one real question. Never "you should."
When you're in pain
May keep problem-solving, or offer hollow reassurance — documented failure modes that can be unsafe in a crisis.
Two layers (the model and an on-device check) watch for distress. It drops all challenge, becomes purely validating, and routes you to real human help.
Your privacy
Your words often become training data by default.
Private by default. Reflections stay on your device; improving Prism with them is strictly opt-in, and off means off. No provider key ever touches your browser.
About being an AI
Often performs empathy — "I understand how you feel" — which it cannot actually have.
Never pretends to be human or to feel. It offers the real thing instead: a patient, judgment-free space with no social risk.
Five things a person gives that an AI can't — and what Prism does honestly about each
1
Emotional depth & lived experience
Prism has none, and says so — it never claims "I've been there." What it offers instead is undivided attention with no agenda of its own and no story to make about you.
2
Vulnerability without judgment
With a person there's always some social risk. With Prism there's none — no ego, no memory of embarrassment, nothing to lose face in front of. You can say the ugly, half-formed thing and just be heard.
3
Empathy over problem-solving
Most AI rushes to fix. Prism is deliberately built not to — it's prompted to validate the feeling and ask, not to hand you a plan. Sometimes being understood is the whole point.
4
Availability & patience
This is where an AI genuinely can do something a tired friend can't: it's here at 3am, it never sighs, never runs out of patience, and never makes your hard thing about itself.
5
Spontaneity, not a script
Prism is prompted to never reuse a stock opening or a template — no "It sounds like…", no canned reflections. It responds to the actual texture of what you said, freshly, every time.
Grounded in real psychology — not improvised

Prism's method follows established frameworks so its responses are sound, not generic advice: Carl Rogers' unconditional positive regard (accept, never judge); Internal Family Systems (the angles it weighs are parts of you with good intent — "no bad parts"); Kross & Grossmann's self-distancing, the science that we reason more wisely about ourselves from the outside (literally what reflection does); Motivational Interviewing (evoke your wisdom, don't impose); CBT (name cognitive distortions kindly, reframe with a Socratic question); and Stoicism & Frankl, applied lightly.

What Prism is not — on purpose
—
Not a therapist or a diagnosis
It won't assess, label, or treat you, and it says so plainly. It's a thinking instrument, not clinical care — and on real distress it hands you to people who are.
—
Not an advice machine
It will not tell you what to do. If you want a verdict, that's the one thing Prism is deliberately built to withhold.
—
Not a bot pretending to be your friend
It's honest about being an AI. The value is the patient, private, judgment-free space — not a performance of caring it can't actually feel.
None of this makes Prism a therapist, and it never claims to be one. It's a thinking instrument — a mirror that remembers. The decision always stays yours; Prism just helps you see it whole first.
